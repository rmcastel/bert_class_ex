  (b)(1)  Examples of reasonable expectation of actual notice.  You may reasonably expect that a consumer will receive actual notice if you:  (i)  Hand-deliver a printed copy of the notice to the consumer;  (ii)  Mail a printed copy of the notice to the last known address of the consumer;  (iii)  For the consumer who conducts transactions electronically, post the notice on the electronic site and require the consumer to acknowledge receipt of the notice as a necessary step to obtaining a particular financial product or service; or  (iv)  For an isolated transaction with the consumer, such as an ATM transaction, post the notice on the ATM screen and require the consumer to acknowledge receipt of the notice as a necessary step to obtaining the particular financial product or service.  (2)  Examples of unreasonable expectation of actual notice.  You may not, however, reasonably expect that a consumer will receive actual notice of your privacy policies and practices if you:  (i)  Only post a sign in your branch or office or generally publish advertisements of your privacy policies and practices; or  (ii)  Send the notice via electronic mail to a consumer who does not obtain a financial product or service from you electronically.
