When you establish a customer relationship General rule You establish a customer relationship when you and the consumer enter into a continuing relationship Special rule for loans You establish a customer relationship with a consumer when you originate a loan to the consumer for personal family or household purposes If you subsequently transfer the servicing rights to that loan to another financial institution the customer relationship transfers with the servicing rights Examples of establishing customer relationship You establish a customer relationship when the consumer Opens a credit card account with you Executes the contract to open a deposit account with you obtains credit from you or purchases insurance from you Agrees to obtain financial economic or investment advisory services from you for a fee or Becomes your client for the purpose of your providing credit counseling or tax preparation services Examples of loan rule You establish a customer relationship with a consumer who obtains a loan for personal family or household purposes when you Originate the loan to the consumer or Purchase the servicing rights to the consumer s loan
